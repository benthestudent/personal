Gymnify Door Scanner

Developed for Gymnify, a 24-hour family oriented gym located in Alabama, this device utilizes a Raspberry Pi to automate the client check-in process. The scanner allows clients to scan their membership cards, which it then uses to verify that the client is a paid-member. If the client has a valid membership, it opens an electric lock, allowing them access to the gym. The scanner will also display a message to the client, which can be customized by the gym owner. In addition to running 24/7, these systems download and constantly update a local version of the user database to ensure continued functionality in case of internet connectivity problems. In service now for roughly three years, these devices have scanned over 180,000 QR/Barcodes and are still in use today.

Replant Earth

I helped develop this website and app at my first ever hackathon, the Atoms and Bits Hackathon at Make School in San Francisco. Inspired by my participation in school strikes against climate change, I wanted to create a simple website and app that would allow anyone to help reverse the destructive effects of global warming by replanting trees around the world. By repeatedly clicking a button or answering trivia questions on the website and app, users would water a virtual tree and thereby interact with ads which generate revenue to be donated to tree-planting nonprofits across the globe. With simplicity and efficiency in mind, this service is meant to enable anyone and everyone to contribute to the efforts against climate change by combating deforestation, which is responsible for cutting down over 15 billion trees a year. Because of its potential to help our society, Replant Earth (originally Treology) won my team the Make School Social Impact award at the hackathon.
